Data Literacy

I do not just pull reports.
I read them.

The difference between someone who runs an SEO report and someone who understands SEO is knowing what the data means and what to do next. I track impressions, clicks, queries, indexation and engagement data across both client projects. Here is what that looks like in practice.

📈

Impressions and Click Rate

I look at the ratio between impressions and clicks to identify which pages have visibility but are not earning clicks. Those pages need meta title improvements, not more content.

🔍

Query Analysis

Reading the queries tab in GSC tells you what Google actually thinks your pages are about. Unexpected queries showing up are new content opportunities. Missing target keywords are gaps to fix.

📑

Page Level Performance

I track which pages are gaining traction and which are sitting at low positions with high impressions. Pages in positions 5 to 15 with existing impressions are the fastest ranking wins to prioritise.

🔗

Internal Linking Gaps

Cross referencing page performance data with site architecture lets me identify which pages are under linked internally. Fixing that is often faster than building new content.

⚙️

Indexation Monitoring

I check the GSC Coverage report regularly to catch pages that are not being indexed. Knowing why something is not indexed is just as important as knowing that it is not.
